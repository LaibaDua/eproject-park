Yellowstone National Park

Mammals

The American badger (Taxidea taxus) is a burrowing mammal found in Yellowstone's grasslands and sagebrush areas.

The bobcat (Lynx rufus) is a versatile wildcat found throughout Yellowstone's diverse habitats, including forests and grasslands

The coyote (Canis latrans) is a highly adaptable predator found throughout Yellowstone, inhabiting forests, grasslands, and valleys.

The gray wolf (Canis lupus) is a keystone predator reintroduced to Yellowstone in 1995, significantly impacting the park's ecosystem.

The long-tailed weasel (Neogale frenata) is a small, agile predator found in Yellowstone's forests, grasslands, and wetlands.

The American marten (Martes americana) is a tree-dwelling carnivore found in Yellowstone's dense coniferous forests.

The short-tailed weasel (Mustela erminea), also known as the ermine, is a small carnivore found in Yellowstone's forests and grasslands.

The wolverine (Gulo gulo) is a rare and elusive carnivore found in Yellowstone's remote alpine and forested areas.

The Canada lynx (Lynx canadensis) is a medium-sized wild cat native to North America, known for its thick fur, tufted ears, and large, snowshoe-like paws.






Ungulates

The bighorn sheep (Ovis canadensis) is a mountainous herbivore found in Yellowstone's rocky cliffs and steep slopes.

The bison (Bison bison) is a symbol of Yellowstone, where it roams the park's grasslands and valleys.

The elk (Cervus elaphus) is one of Yellowstone's most abundant large mammals, found in the park's forests and meadows.

The mountain goat (Oreamnos americanus) is an introduced species in the Yellowstone region, inhabiting steep, rocky alpine terrain.

White-tailed deer (Odocoileus virginianus) are a lesser-seen species in Yellowstone National Park, primarily inhabiting lower elevations with forest edges and riparian zones.






Rodents

The beaver (Castor canadensis) is a keystone species in Yellowstone, often found in wetlands and riparian zones.

The golden-mantled ground squirrel (Spermophilus lateralis) is a small rodent found in Yellowstone's forests and meadows.

The montane vole (Microtus montanus) is a small rodent found in Yellowstone's meadows and grasslands.

The yellow-bellied marmot (Marmota flaviventris) is a large ground squirrel found in Yellowstone's alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

The least chipmunk (Tamias minimus) is a small, striped rodent found in Yellowstone's forests, grasslands, and rocky areas.
