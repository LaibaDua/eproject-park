Reptiles and Amphibians
Boorolong fROGThe Sumatran lion does not exist as a real animal species. Lions (Panthera leo) are native to Africa and a small population exists in India (Asiatic lions). Sumatra, an island in Indonesia, is home to unique wildlife, including the Sumatran tiger (Panthera tigris sumatrae), but no lions.
TuataraGiraffes are not native to Sumatra or any part of Southeast Asia. They are native to Africa and are the tallest land animals, belonging to the species Giraffa camelopardalis. Giraffes are known for their long necks, which help them browse leaves from tall trees, primarily acacia. Their unique coat patterns act as camouflage and are distinct for each individual.
AllugatorThe binturong (Arctictis binturong), also known as the "bearcat," is a medium-sized mammal native to Southeast Asia, including Sumatra. Despite its nickname, it is neither a bear nor a cat but belongs to the civet family (Viverridae).
Bellinger River TurtleThe pygmy hippopotamus (Choeropsis liberiensis or Hexaprotodon liberiensis) is a small species of hippopotamus native to West Africa, primarily found in countries like Liberia, Sierra Leone, Guinea, and Ivory Coast. It is distinct from the larger common hippopotamus (Hippopotamus amphibius).
Eyelash Viper BodyThe eastern bongo (Tragelaphus eurycerus isaaci) is a subspecies of bongo, a large and strikingly beautiful antelope native to the forests of East Africa. It is also known as the mountain bongo due to its habitat in high-altitude montane forests.
Yellow Spotted BellThe Yellow-spotted Bell Frog (Litoria castanea), also known as the Yellow-spotted Tree Frog, is a critically endangered species endemic to southeastern Australia.
Pygmy PythonThe Pygmy Python (Antaresia perthensis), also known as the Anthill Python, holds the title of the world's smallest python species, reaching a maximum length of just 62 cm.
Frilled LizardThe Frilled Lizard (Chlamydosaurus kingii), also known as the Frill-necked Lizard, is a fascinating reptile native to northern Australia and southern New Guinea. Known for its dramatic neck frill, this species is an iconic example of Australia’s unique wildlife.
Veiled ChameleonThe Veiled Chameleon (Chamaeleo calyptratus) is a striking and fascinating reptile known for its ability to change color and its unique physical adaptations. It is native to the mountainous regions of Yemen and Saudi Arabia.





Mammals
American Lion:The American lion (Panthera atrox), also known as the North American lion or Naheho’ bahamute (by some Native American tribes), is an extinct species of large cat that lived during the Pleistocene epoch (about 340,000 to 11,000 years ago). It is considered one of the largest felids to have ever existed.
Asian Elephant:The Asian elephant (Elephas maximus) is the largest land animal in Asia and is a keystone species in the ecosystems it inhabits. It is smaller than the African elephant and is native to the forests and grasslands of South and Southeast Asia, including India, Sri Lanka, Nepal, Myanmar, Thailand, and Indonesia.
Squirrel Monkey:The squirrel monkey (Saimiri spp.) is a small, agile primate known for its playful nature and distinctive appearance. They are native to the tropical forests of Central and South America.
Chimpanzee:The chimpanzee (Pan troglodytes) is one of the closest living relatives to humans, sharing about 98% of our DNA. They are highly intelligent, social primates found in the forests and savannas of West and Central Africa.
Red Kangaroo:The red kangaroo (Osphranter rufus) is the largest marsupial and one of Australia's most iconic animals. It is well-adapted to the arid and semi-arid regions of the continent, thriving in open plains and deserts.





Birds
Superb fruit dove:Tiny rainforest dove with white belly and white vent (compared to Rose-crowned Fruit-Dove which has a yellow belly and vent).
Noisy pitta:The adult Noisy Pitta has a mostly black head and neck but the crown of its head is chestnut brown with a central black stripe.
Forest kingfisher:It is a predominantly blue and white bird. It is found in Indonesia, New Guinea and coastal eastern and Northern Australia.
Barking Owl:They are a medium-sized brown owl and have a characteristic voice with calls ranging from a barking dog noise to an intense human-like howl.
Pied heron:The pied heron (Egretta picata ), also known as the pied egret is a bird found in coastal and subcoastal areas of monsoonal northern Australia as well as some parts of Wallacea and New Guinea.
