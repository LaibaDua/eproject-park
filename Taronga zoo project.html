Taronga Zoo National Park

Taronga Zoo National Park Information

Welcome to Taronga Zoo National Park
Taronga Zoo, located in Mosman on Sydney Harbour, Australia, is a renowned zoological park that opened on October 7, 1916. Covering approximately 28 hectares, it is home to over 5,000 animals across 350 species.

A Glimpse into History
Taronga Zoo, located in Mosman, Sydney, Australia, opened on October 7, 1916. It was established by the Taronga Park Trust and was initially created as part of a larger plan to conserve wildlife. The zoo spans 28 hectares and is home to over 5,000 animals from 350 species. It has grown to become one of the most well-known zoos in the world, focusing on both animal conservation and environmental education. Over the years, it has expanded its facilities and exhibits, becoming a key player in global wildlife preservation efforts.

What Makes Taronga Zoo Special
Tarango Zoo boasts an array of attractions that captivate millions of visitors each year:

  • Stunning Location: Situated on the edge of Sydney Harbour, it offers breathtaking views of the harbor and the Sydney Opera House, making it one of the most scenic zoos in the world.
  • Conservation Efforts: The zoo plays a major role in global wildlife conservation, supporting over 50 wildlife conservation projects both in Australia and internationally.
  • Diverse Wildlife: Taronga Zoo is home to over 5,000 animals from 350 species, including iconic Australian animals like kangaroos and koalas, as well as exotic species like tigers, elephants, and chimpanzees.
