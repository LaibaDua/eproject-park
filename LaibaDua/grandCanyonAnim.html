Mammals
MOUNTAIN LIONS: Mountain lions, also known as cougars or pumas, are large, solitary wild cats native to the Americas. They are agile predators, known for their ability to leap great distances.
COYOTES: Coyotes are highly adaptable, medium-sized canids found across North America, known for their intelligence and resourcefulness. They are omnivores, thriving in various habitats.
BOBCATS: Bobcats are elusive, medium-sized wild cats native to North America, recognizable by their tufted ears and short, bobbed tails. They are skilled hunters, preying on small animals.
ELK: Elk are large, majestic members of the deer family, found in forests and grasslands of North America and parts of Asia. Known for their impressive antlers and bugling calls.
MULL DEER: Mule deer are graceful deer species native to western North America, easily identified by their large, mule-like ears.





Birds
PEREGRINE FALCONS: Peregrine falcons are powerful birds of prey known for being the fastest animals on Earth, reaching speeds of over 240 mph during their stoops.
CALIFORNIA CONDORS: California condors are critically endangered vultures, known for their impressive 10-foot wingspan, making them one of the largest flying birds in North America.
RAVENS: Ravens are intelligent and adaptable birds, known for their glossy black feathers and complex vocalizations. Found worldwide, they are problem-solvers and often associated with myths and folklore due to their mysterious nature.
WESTERN BLUEBIRDS: Western bluebirds are small, brightly colored songbirds with vivid blue feathers and rusty-orange chests. Found in open woodlands and grasslands, they are insectivorous and often nest in tree cavities or birdhouses.
TURKEY VULTURES: Turkey vultures are large, scavenging birds recognized by their red heads and broad, dark wings with silvery undersides.




Reptiles
GILA MONSTER: The Gila monster is a venomous, brightly colored lizard native to the deserts of the southwestern United States and Mexico. Known for its slow movement and distinct orange or pink patterns.
GOPHER SNAKE: Gopher snakes are non-venomous, ground-dwelling snakes found in North America, known for their defensive hissing when threatened.
SHORT-HORNED LIZARD: Short-horned lizards, also known as horny toads, are small, stout reptiles with spiny bodies and short horns on their heads. Native to North America, they are well-adapted to desert environments.
RATTLE SNAKE: Rattlesnakes are venomous snakes, easily recognized by the rattle at the end of their tails, which they use as a warning sound. Found across the Americas, they are skilled hunters.
YELLOW-BACKED SPINY LIZARD: The yellow-backed spiny lizard is a colorful reptile native to the southwestern United States, known for its striking yellow and blue patterns on its back.
