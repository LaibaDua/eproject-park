The Bigs Nine
AFRICANLION:African lions have been admired throughout history for as symbols of courage and strength. These iconic animals have powerful bodies—in the cat family, they're second in size only to tigers—and lion roars that can be heard from five miles away.
CHIMPANZEE: Like us, chimps are highly social animals, care for their offspring for years and can live to be over 50. In fact, chimpanzees are our closest cousins; we share about 98% of our genes. In their habitat in the forests of Central Africa, chimpanzees spend most of their days in the treetops.
RHINOO:A rhinoceros is a large, herbivorous mammal known for its thick skin and one or two horns on its snout. Found primarily in Africa and Asia, rhinos are endangered due to poaching and habitat loss.
ELEPHANT The elephant is the world's largest living creature. - The elephant is a species that is both intelligent and obedient. - The elephant is a four-legged creature with two small eyes, two large ears, a trunk, and a short tail. - The elephant's four legs are all thick and big..
SUN BEAR: Sun bears are named for the golden patches of fur on their chests, While their tongues may be extra-big, sun bears are actually the smallest bear species. Males grow about 5 feet long and weigh up to 150 pounds—that's about half the size of an adult American black bear.
PANDA:A panda is a bear species native to China, known for its distinctive black-and-white fur. It is primarily herbivorous, with bamboo making up the majority of its diet, and is classified as vulnerable due to habitat loss and low reproductive rates.
RACONDOG: The common raccoon dog, also called the Chinese or Asian raccoon dog to distinguish it from the Japanese raccoon dog, is a small, heavy-set, fox-like canid native to East Asia. Named for its raccoon-like face markings, it is most closely related to foxes.
WHITE TIGER: The white tiger is a result of a rare genetic mutation and the most efficient way to breed them is by using two tigers who have the recessive genes needed to produce offspring with a white coat.
ZEBRA: Zebras are equids, members of the horse family. They have excellent hearing and eyesight and can run at speeds of up to 35 miles per hour (56 kilometers per hour). They also have a powerful kick that can cause serious injury to a predator, like a lion, a hyena, or an African wild dog.

Mammals
MEERKATS: Meerkats are small, social mammals native to southern Africa, known for their upright posture and cooperative behavior within groups. They live in large colonies and are famous for their vigilant guarding against predators.
ORANGUTANS: Orangutans are great apes found in the rainforests of Borneo and Sumatra, known for their reddish-brown fur and exceptional intelligence. They are mostly solitary, spending much of their time in trees and using tools to gather food.
RACONDOG: The common raccoon dog, also called the Chinese or Asian raccoon dog to distinguish it from the Japanese raccoon dog, is a small, heavy-set, fox-like canid native to East Asia. Named for its raccoon-like face markings, it is most closely related to foxes.
OTEERS: Otters are carnivorous mammals in the subfamily Lutrinae. The 13 extant otter species are all semiaquatic , aquatic, or marine. Lutrinae is a branch of the Mustelidae family, which includes weasels, badgers, mink, and wolverines, among other animals.
KOALOS: The koala, sometimes inaccurately called the koala bear, is an arboreal herbivorous marsupial native to Australia. It is the only extant representative of the family Phascolarctidae. Its closest living relatives are the wombats




Mammals
JAVANMYNA: The Javan myna, also known as the white-vented myna, is a species of myna. It is a member of the starling family. It is native to Bali and Java. It has been introduced to other Asian countries, and as far away as Puerto Rico.
CRIMSON SUNBIRD:The crimson sunbird is a species of bird in the sunbird family which feed largely on nectar . They may also take insects, especially when feeding their young. Flight is fast and direct on their short wings. Most species can take nectar by hovering like a hummingbird, but usually perch to feed.
ASHYTAILOR:The ashy tailorbird is a species of bird formerly placed in the "Old World warbler" assemblage, it but now placed in the family Cisticolidae. It is found in Brunei, Indonesia, Malaysia, Myanmar, the Philippines, Singapore, and Thailand, Vietnam
ZEBRA DOVE:The zebra dove, also known as the barred ground dove, or barred dove, is a species of bird of the dove family, Columbidae, native to Southeast Asia. They are small birds with a long tail, predominantly brownish-grey in colour with black-and-white barring
The pink-necked green pigeon (Treron vernans) is a species of bird of the pigeon and dove family, Columbidae. It is a common species of Southeast Asia
