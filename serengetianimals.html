Serengeti National Park

Animals & Wildlife in Serengeti
THE BIG FIVE

The lion (Panthera leo) is one of the most iconic predators in the Serengeti National Park, located in Tanzania. This park is part of the larger Serengeti ecosystem, renowned for its abundant wildlife and the annual Great Migration.

The African elephant (Loxodonta africana) is a majestic and vital species found in the Serengeti National Park, Tanzania. These elephants are the largest land mammals on Earth and play a key ecological role in the Serengeti ecosystem.

The rhinoceros (Diceros bicornis for black rhino, Ceratotherium simum for white rhino) is a rare and critically endangered species found in the Serengeti National Park, though sightings are infrequent due to their low numbers and elusive nature.

The leopard (Panthera pardus) is a stealthy and elusive predator found in the Serengeti National Park. It is one of the "Big Five" animals and a key part of the Serengeti ecosystem.

The African buffalo (Syncerus caffer), also known as the Cape buffalo, is a powerful and social herbivore commonly found in the Serengeti National Park. It is one of the "Big Five" animals and a critical species in the Serengeti ecosystem.






Mammals

The aardvark (Orycteropus afer), a nocturnal and elusive mammal, is found in the Serengeti National Park, though it is rarely seen due to its secretive nature.

The aardwolf (Proteles cristata) is a small, insectivorous mammal found in the Serengeti National Park. It is a member of the hyena family but has distinct behaviors and a specialized diet that set it apart.

The African hare (Lepus microtis), also known as the scrub hare or African savanna hare, is a small, swift mammal commonly found in the Serengeti National Park and throughout much of sub-Saharan Africa.

Antelope:The term "antelope" refers to a diverse group of herbivorous mammals found throughout Africa, including in the Serengeti National Park.

The banded mongoose (Mungos mungo) is a small, social carnivore found in the Serengeti National Park, where it thrives in the open grasslands and savanna ecosystems.






Birds

Abdim's stork (Ciconia abdimii) is a striking bird commonly found in the Serengeti National Park and other parts of East and Central Africa. Known for its black body and white wings, it is one of the more notable species of stork in the region.

The African darter (Anhinga rufa) is a large water bird commonly found in the Serengeti National Park, particularly near lakes, rivers, and wetlands.

The African grey hornbill (Tockus nasutus) is a medium-sized bird native to sub-Saharan Africa, including the Serengeti National Park

The African fish eagle (Haliaeetus vocifer) is a large and powerful bird of prey found across sub-Saharan Africa, including the Serengeti National Park.

The African harrier hawk is a fascinating and versatile bird of prey, known for its distinctive hunting style and adaptability. In the Serengeti, it contributes to the rich diversity of raptors and plays an important role in maintaining ecological balance.
