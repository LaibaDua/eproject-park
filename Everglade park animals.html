The Big five
American Alligator:The American Alligator (Alligator mississippiensis) is a large reptile native to the southeastern United States. It is typically found in freshwater environments like rivers, lakes, and swamps. Males can grow up to 13-15 feet long, while females are smaller, reaching around 9-10 feet. These alligators are apex predators, feeding on fish, birds, mammals, and occasionally larger prey. They are known for their powerful bite, thick armor-like skin, and their ability to regulate their body temperature through basking in the sun. They were once endangered due to hunting but have made a remarkable recovery through conservation efforts.
American Crocodile:The American Crocodile (Crocodylus acutus) is a large reptile found in coastal areas of the southeastern United States, particularly in Florida, as well as parts of Central and South America and the Caribbean. Unlike alligators, American crocodiles prefer saltwater or brackish habitats, such as estuaries, mangrove swamps, and coastal rivers. They are generally lighter in color, with a pale gray-green appearance.
Florida Panther:The Florida Panther (Puma concolor coryi) is a subspecies of cougar found primarily in the forests and swamps of southern Florida, USA. It is a medium-sized wild cat, with males typically weighing between 120-160 pounds and females around 60-100 pounds. Their coat is tawny with lighter undersides, and they have a long, muscular tail.
Bottlenose Dolphin:The Bottlenose Dolphin (Tursiops truncatus) is one of the most well-known and widely distributed species of dolphin. They are found in warm and temperate seas worldwide, including coastal areas and offshore waters. Bottlenose dolphins are highly intelligent and social animals, living in groups called pods, which can range from a few individuals to over 100 dolphins.
Roseate SpoonbillThe Roseate Spoonbill (Platalea ajaja) is a striking wader bird found in coastal habitats, such as wetlands, mangroves, and mudflats, throughout the Americas, particularly in the southeastern United States, Central America, and parts of South America. Known for its vibrant pink plumage, which is a result of the carotenoid pigments in its diet, the Roseate Spoonbill has a distinctive spoon-shaped bill that it uses to forage for food in shallow waters.

Mammals
GRANT'S GAZELLE:Grant's Gazelle (Nanger granti) is a species of antelope found in East Africa, primarily in Kenya, Tanzania, and parts of Uganda. It is one of the most well-known and widespread species of gazelle, typically found in savannas, grasslands, and open woodlands.
SPOTTED HYENA:The Spotted Hyena (Crocuta crocuta) is a large carnivorous mammal found throughout sub-Saharan Africa. It is one of the most well-known and socially complex predators in the animal kingdom, known for its distinctive "laugh," which is actually a series of vocalizations used for communication within the clan.
CRESTED PORCUPINE:The Crested Porcupine (Hystrix cristata) is a large species of porcupine found in parts of North Africa and the Middle East, including countries like Morocco, Egypt, and Israel. It is notable for its distinctive crest of long, spiny quills that extend from the back of its head, giving it a unique appearance.
AARDWOLF:The Aardwolf (Proteles cristata) is a nocturnal mammal native to the savannas and grasslands of sub-Saharan Africa. It is a small, insectivorous member of the hyena family, though it differs from the more well-known spotted hyena in both size and diet.
HONEY BADGER:The Honey Badger (Mellivora capensis) is a small but fearless carnivore found throughout sub-Saharan Africa, the Middle East, and parts of Asia. It is known for its boldness and tenacity, often tackling prey much larger than itself or defending itself aggressively against potential threats. Despite its name, the honey badger is not closely related to the European badger, and its diet is much more varied.





Birds
Roseate Spoonbill:The Roseate Spoonbill (Platalea ajaja) is a striking wader bird found in coastal habitats, such as wetlands, mangroves, and mudflats, throughout the Americas, particularly in the southeastern United States, Central America, and parts of South America. Known for its vibrant pink plumage, which is a result of the carotenoid pigments in its diet, the Roseate Spoonbill has a distinctive spoon-shaped bill that it uses to forage for food in shallow waters.
Great Blue Heron:The Great Blue Heron (Ardea herodias) is a large wading bird native to North and Central America, found in a variety of wetland habitats, including lakes, rivers, marshes, and coastal areas. It is the largest heron species in North America, known for its impressive size, elegant appearance, and hunting skills.
Anhinga:The Anhinga (Anhinga anhinga), also known as the "snakebird" or "water turkey," is a large water bird native to the Americas, found in freshwater habitats such as lakes, rivers, and wetlands. Its distinctive appearance and impressive diving abilities make it easily recognizable.
Wood Stork:The Wood Stork (Mycteria americana) is a large wading bird native to the southeastern United States, Central America, and parts of South America. It is one of the few stork species found in North America and is easily recognizable due to its distinctive appearance.
Snail Kite:The Snail Kite (Rostrhamus sociabilis) is a medium-sized raptor found in freshwater wetlands across parts of the Americas, particularly in southern Florida, Central America, and parts of South America. It is known for its specialized diet and unique hunting style.
